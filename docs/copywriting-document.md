# Copywriting Voice & Content

## Tone
Unhurried. Confident. Never boastful. Like a bartender who has seen everything and is impressed by nothing — except this drink.

## Rules
- Avoid exclamation marks.
- Avoid superlatives without substance.
- Avoid generic luxury words like "premium" or "exquisite".
- Use short declarative sentences.
- Use sensory language (smell, texture, temperature).
- Implied intimacy — "you", not "our customers".

## Page Copy

### Hero
- Eyebrow: EST. 1965 — THE ORIGINAL INDULGENCE
- Headline: Some drinks are ordered. This one is savored.
- Subheadline: Kahlúa. Vodka. Heavy cream. Three ingredients. One ritual.

### Quote
"The White Russian asks nothing of you. It only asks that you slow down." — Jeff Bridges, probably

### Ingredients Section
- Kahlúa: Roasted arabica, dark rum, vanilla. The soul of the glass.
- Vodka: Crystal-clear spirit. The invisible backbone.
- Heavy Cream: Room temperature for the perfect float. The silk layer.

### Sample Lines
- Heavy cream doesn't float. It hovers. Waiting.
- Vodka doesn't ask for attention. That's why it's here.
- Made in under three minutes. Best experienced in twenty.
